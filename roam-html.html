About Roam



Travelling is good for the soul. People like having small adventures to nearby locations. However, sometimes options can seem limited. As a tool to overcome filter-bubble and fit adventures of any length into our schedules, Roam was developed.

In the future, we hope to develop Roam into a site where people can store their previous destinations if desired, write a little bit about how the adventure went, and share with friends. We could futhermore potentially display the stories you write about your adventures on our homepage, and develop a culture of local Roamers. <3

We also hope to implement real walk-time maps into our algorithm, to give a more accurate time estimate of your adventures.

If you'd like to see these developments happen soon, click here!





Why Roam?

Burst Your Bubble

“For the past two years I’ve been letting randomized computer programs decide what I do. I believe giving in to chance is a way to liberate yourself from personal and social programming that traps you in a narrow sense of self.”

- Max Hawkins, nomadic programmer

We were inspired by Max Hawkins’ philosophy on choice. Our random walking site draws heavily on his work!



All Dressed Up with Nowhere to Go

We wanted places to discover on Friday nights. Choosing on Yelp or Google was too difficult and limiting for us. We often felt like we needed a ‘reason’ to ‘go somewhere’, rather than seeing the act of walking as the adventure itself. Roaming allowed us to shift to a different, more experience-focused mindset.



Befriend Your Area

Roaming is a way to become more familiar with the minutia of a place. Walking can develop a different, slower, and perhaps richer relationship to a place. Also, sometimes we discovered cool secret places on our walks.



Ditch the Car

As students living in a spread-out, car-culture city, it felt like we needed the convenience of a car to do anything fun. However, as we walked more in the surrounding area, we saw the journey itself as the destination, and then cars seemed less important. We were also using a more diversified transportation system for our adventurous outings. We developed a small but strong pedestrian community, while reducing reliance on carbon-emitting vehicles! As an added bonus, we exercised our bodies (and spirits!).
